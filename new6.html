Email Writing & Phone Etiquette

What I Learn!

Email Etiquette

1.1 Use a Professional Email Address

Avoid nicknames. Use formats like: firstname.lastname@email.com

1.2 Clear Subject Line

The subject should show the purpose. Examples:

  • Request for Appointment
  • Submission of Assignment

1.3 Use Proper Greetings

Start with:

  • "Dear Sir/Madam"
  • "Dear [Name]"

1.4 Keep the Message Clear & Short

Use simple sentences, bullet points, and short paragraphs.

1.5 Professional Tone

No emojis, slang, or jokes.

1.6 Check Grammar & Spelling

Proof-read before sending.

1.7 Proper Closing

Use:

  • "Thank You"
  • "Best regards,"

1.8 Use CC/BCC Correctly

Add only necessary recipients.

1.9 Reply Promptly

Respond within 24 hours. Even a short reply is fine: "Received. Will respond shortly."

Phone Etiquette

2.1 Answer Professionally

Greet politely:

  • "Good morning, this is [Your Name] speaking."

2.2 Speak Clearly & Politely

Use a calm voice; avoid shouting or speaking too fast.

2.3 Listen Carefully

Don't interrupt; let the caller finish.

2.4 Avoid Background Noise

Answer in a quiet place.

2.5 Be Respectful

Use polite phrases:

  • "May I know who is speaking?"
  • "Could you please repeat that?"

2.6 If You Need to Put Them on Hold

Ask first:

  • "May I place you on hold for a moment?" Don't keep them waiting too long.

2.7 Take Notes

Write important details, especially in work-related calls.

2.8 End Politely

"Thank you for calling. Have a nice day."

Importance Of Email & Phone Etiquette

  • Shows professionalism and respect.
  • Clear communication avoids misunderstandings.
  • Proper greetings, tone, grammar, and structure are essential.
  • Listening is as important as speaking in phone calls.
  • Both require politeness and clarity at all times.

Job Interview Mastery

1. What is a Job Interview?

A conversation between you and the company to check:

  • Your skills, experience, personality
  • Whether you are the right match for the job
  • You also check if the job suits you

2. Best Interview Strategy

Ask yourself:

  • What do I want the interviewer to know about me?
  • What weaknesses should I hide or minimize?
  • What do I need to know about the company?

3. Communication Skills (Very Important!)

Interview success depends on communication:

  • Non-verbal (93%): Body language (55%) + Tone of voice (38%)
  • Words only 7%

4. Non-Verbal Skills

  • Smile and greet
  • Maintain eye contact
  • Sit straight
  • Don't show nervousness
  • Show enthusiasm
  • Read interviewer's body cues

5. Successful Candidates Do This

  • Use company name
  • Show research
  • Use specific examples
  • Use technical terms
  • Ask questions
  • Make eye contact
  • Speak more than interviewer
  • Give clear, confident answers

6. Active Listening Tips

  • Pay attention
  • Show that you listen
  • Ask for clarification
  • Don't judge
  • Respond nicely

7. 6 C's of Communication (+1)

  • Clear · Concise · Correct · Complete · Concrete · Courteous · Confident

8. How to Describe Yourself

  • Personality/Strengths: "I'm good at meeting deadlines", "My strength is communication"
  • Education: "I have a diploma in…", "I graduated from…"
  • Experience: "I have 2 years of experience in…", "I worked as…"
  • Future Goals: "I want to further my career in…", "I can contribute by…"

9. Interview Answer Structure (STAR Method)

  1. Introduce your example
  2. Explain what your goal was
  3. Explain what actions you took
  4. Explain the result (success)

10. Other Interview Basics

  • Wear: Formal, neat
  • Bring: CV, certificates, notebook
  • Location: Know where to go before
  • Practice: Do mock interviews!

Portfolio Management Mastery

1. What is a Portfolio?

A portfolio is a collection of your work, achievements, skills, and documents used to show your abilities to others.

2. Uses of a Portfolio

  • Job applications – to show skills and past work
  • Career promotions – to prove achievements
  • Creative fields – design, writing, photography
  • Education – show learning progress
  • University admissions – show talents and activities
  • Teaching – collect lesson plans, student work
  • Freelancing & consulting – show past projects
  • Business & entrepreneurship – show plans and products
  • Networking & personal branding
  • Career change – show transferable skills

3. Types of Portfolios

  1. Personal Portfolio
    Shows your creative work (art, design, writing, photography).
  2. Career / Dossier Portfolio
    Includes resume, cover letter, work documents.
  3. Employment Portfolio
    Shows your workplace achievements, certificates, performance.
  4. Assessment Portfolio
    Shows your learning progress, assignments, projects (used in schools/universities).

4. How to Create an Assessment Portfolio (17 Steps)

  1. Know your purpose
  2. Know your audience
  3. Choose format – physical or digital
  4. Collect materials – certificates, work, projects
  5. Organize sections – intro, CV, education, work, skills
  6. Give context – explain each item
  7. Highlight achievements
  8. Keep it professional and neat
  9. Add reflections – strengths, weaknesses
  10. Update regularly
  11. Ask for feedback
  12. Customize for each purpose
  13. Practice presenting
  14. Keep backups
  15. Protect privacy
  16. Use digital tools smartly
  17. Keep improving

Mastering Confident & Effective Speaking in Meetings

1. Meeting Terminology

  • Agenda – List of topics to discuss
  • Minutes – Written record of what happened
  • Objectives – Goals of the meeting
  • Facilitator/Chairperson – Person who leads the meeting
  • Participants – People attending
  • Action Items – Tasks assigned
  • AOB (Any Other Business) – Topics at the end

2. Types of Meetings

  • Staff Meeting – Regular team updates
  • Project Meeting – Discuss project progress
  • Brainstorming Session – Generate new ideas
  • Problem-Solving Meeting – Fix specific issues
  • Annual General Meeting (AGM) – Yearly formal meeting

3. How to Plan a Meeting (Checklist)

  • What is the purpose of the meeting?
  • Who needs to attend?
  • What equipment is required?
  • Is it formal or informal?
  • What documents to prepare? (Agenda, previous minutes)
  • How long should it last?

4. How to Contribute Effectively

  • Speak clearly and confidently
  • Use short and direct sentences
  • Support points with examples
  • Listen actively before responding
  • Be respectful even when disagreeing

5. Meeting Roles & Responsibilities

  • Chairperson – Opens, leads, closes meeting
  • Secretary – Takes minutes, distributes agenda
  • Timekeeper – Ensures schedule is followed
  • Presenter – Shares reports/data
  • Participant – Contributes ideas

6. Writing a Professional Meeting Agenda

  • Title, date, time, location
  • List topics in order
  • Include time allocation
  • State purpose (Inform/Discuss/Decide)
  • Attach supporting documents

7. Facilitating a Meeting Effectively

  • Start and end on time
  • Encourage everyone to speak
  • Keep discussion on track
  • Summarize key points
  • Handle conflicts calmly

8. Common Meeting Challenges & Solutions

  • Dominating speakers → Politely interrupt
  • Silent participants → Ask direct questions
  • Off-topic talk → Use "parking lot"
  • Running out of time → Prioritize items

9. Evaluating a Meeting

  • Did we achieve objectives?
  • What went well?
  • What can be improved?
  • Collect quick feedback

Practice → Confidence → Leadership

Writing Professional Project Proposals

1. What is a Project?

A project is a big task that requires:

  • Time
  • Effort
  • Planning

Before starting, you must write a project proposal to explain:

  • What you want to do
  • Why you want to do it
  • How you will do it

2. Types of Project Proposals

  • External Proposal – For outside organizations (win contracts)
  • Internal Proposal – For people inside your company (get approval)

3. Structure of an External Proposal

  • 1. Executive Summary – Short, powerful overview
  • 2. Management Section – Who will work, their skills, timeline
  • 3. Financial Section – Detailed cost breakdown

4. Writing an Internal Proposal

A. Planning the Proposal

  • Understand your audience
  • Know their knowledge level & decision-making power
  • Use visuals (charts, diagrams)
  • Ask: What is the problem? → What is the solution? → Can & should it be done?

B. Writing the Proposal

Start with a strong Introduction that includes:

  • Purpose of the proposal
  • Your credibility
  • Problem background & importance
  • Proposed solution
  • Structure of the document

5. Discussion Section (Main Body)

The heart of persuasion. Include:

  • Detailed problem description
  • Strong, clear solution
  • Implementation schedule
  • People involved
  • Resources needed
  • Expected benefits

6. Summary

A winning project proposal clearly answers:

  • What? – The project
  • Why? – The need/importance
  • How? – Plan, team, budget
  • Who? – Credible team
  • When? – Realistic timeline

A Great Proposal = Approved Project!

Professional Dining Etiquette & Table Manners

1. Why Table Etiquette Matters

  • Shows respect and courtesy
  • People judge your behavior at the table
  • Dining is a social ritual that reflects your upbringing and professionalism

2. Golden Rules (True/False Answers)

  • Never put dirty utensils back on the table
  • Eat French fries with a fork at formal events
  • Never remove food from teeth at the table
  • Don't point out others' mistakes
  • Never rearrange seating cards

3. How to Read a Place Setting

  • Bread plate = left ("b")
  • Drinks = right ("d")
  • Start utensils from the outside → inward

4. Bread & Butter Etiquette

  • Break bread into small pieces
  • Take butter once onto your bread plate
  • Never double-dip into shared butter

5. Salt & Pepper

They are "married" — always pass both together

6. Soup Etiquette

  • Spoon away from you
  • Only sip from cup if no large rim and only broth remains
  • Rest spoon on saucer, not in bowl

7. How to Hold Silverware

  • Continental Style: Don't switch hands, fork tines down
  • American Style: Cut → put knife down → switch fork to right hand

8. Removing Food (U.F.O.)

Use the same utensil that put the food in your mouth. Never hide in napkin.

9. Napkin Rules

  • Place on lap immediately after host
  • Never use as a bib
  • When leaving temporarily: on chair
  • When finished: loosely beside plate

10. Burping, Sneezing & Body Functions

  • Cover mouth with napkin quietly
  • Never blow nose at table
  • Excuse yourself if needed

11. Plate Signals

  • Not Finished (Continental): Inverted "V", fork tines down
  • Not Finished (American): Knife & fork crossed
  • Finished (Both): Knife & fork together at 4:25 position

Master the table → Master the room.

Research skills & Research Design

1.What is Research?

Research is a systematic and organized process of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting information to answer a question or solve a problem.

2.Good Research Study is:

  • Structured
  • Logical
  • Clear
  • Evidence-based

3.what is Research Study Design:

Research design is the overall plan or blueprint of how the research will be carried out.

It's Include:

  • What data is needed
  • How data will be collected
  • How data will be analyzed

Type of Research Design

3.Qualitative Research Design

Focuses on:

  • Opinions
  • Experience
  • Human behavior

Method include

  • Interviews
  • Focus Group
  • observation

3.1 Quantitative Research Design

Focuses on:

  • surveys
  • Experiment
  • Numeric Test

3.2 Mix Method Research

A combination of quantitative and qualitative approaches. Useful when both numerical results and detailed explainations are required

4.Component of Research Proposal writting

  • Title page
  • Table of content
  • introduction
  • problem statement
  • objective
  • literature review
  • Methodology
  • Timeline
  • Budget

Internal proposals are shorter and used within the organizations

External proposals are more detailed and usd when requesting funding or approval

5. Sampling Techniques

Sampling helps select individuals or elements that represent the whole population

  • .Random Sampling: Every member has an equal chance of being selected
  • .Stratified Sampling: Population in divided into groups, and samples are taken from each group
  • .Cluster Sampling: Population is divided into clusters and entire clusters are selected
  • Systematic Sampling: Selecting every nth member from a list

6.Writing an Abstract

We learned that an abstarct should be:

  • short
  • clear
  • About 150-250 Words
  • A summery of entire research

An abstarct usually contains

  • Background
  • Purpose
  • Method
  • Result
  • Conclusion

7.Key Takeaway

  • Research is a structured and systematic way of answering questions.
  • Research design acts as the blueprint for the entire study.
  • Qualitative research = descriptive and exploratory.
  • Quantitative research = numerical data + statistical analysis.
  • Mixed methods combine both qualitative and quantitative approaches to give deeper insights.
  • Sampling techniques help collect accurate and representative data.
  • A good proposal and abstract require clarity, proper structure, and relevance.
